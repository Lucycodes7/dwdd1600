Personal Poster - Apeirophobia

Prompt

Design a functional poster which describes an aspect or characteristic of yourself. Be creative! Take a hard look at yourself (literally and figuratively) and choose some personal attribute or aspect about yourself and “advertise” it with a poster. This poster design is not merely to be the creation of an image as a self portrait, it is the development of a poster to communicate a particular characteristic about yourself creatively and conceptually (in a non-obvious manner). You do not necessarily have to choose a strength or positive theme, but choose something that is unique about yourself. Then think of a creative way to express this uniqueness in an appropriate and artistic poster design.

Artist Statement

The theme of my personal poster revolves around apeirophobia, the fear of infinity, as it is something I have struggled with as an individual. Apeirophobia :is the fear of infinity, and may be manifest as a gnawing fixation on the endlessness of space and the innumerable possibilities of time. I found that infinity is a hard concept to represent but I ended up choosing space to represent infinity as space is also a big aspect of apeirophobia. I wanted to show a woman that represents myself, looking up and pondering. I cut out her eyes and the silhouette of the tears to create a window that shows that space, or infinity is what is behind her thoughts and tears. I also repeated the woman back into space seemingly forever, to represent her going on for infinity, as existing forever is also a great aspect of apeirophobia. For the Text, I chose the word “Infinite”, which can also be read as “In Finite” as I am an evidently finite being trying to comprehend a reality that is seemingly infinite.

Type Face Poster - Gotham

Prompt

Design a poster that conveys a type face of your choosing, and the important information that is associated with the typeface. The Poster should include the following information designed in a grid format.

  • Typeface and typographer in question
  • Biographic info on Typographer
  • Historic info about the typeface, when it was created, maybe why, other interesting facts
  • Type Classification of typeface (serif, san serif, best uses, etc)
  • Any other interesting facts about the typographer or typeface

Artist Statement

This poster was inspired by the typeface Gotham created by Tobias Frere-Jones and Jonathan Hoefler. The typeface was based on the style of type seen all around New York City. I included key information on the typeface and the background of its creators to help create a better understanding and appreciation for the renowned typeface. I chose the colors commonly associated with New York City and I was especially inspired by the colors of Milton Glaser’s “I Heart NY” logo.
